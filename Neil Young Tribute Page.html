Neil Young

Canadian Artistic Legend

Here is a rough time-line of Neil Young's career
  • After dropping out of highschool Neil joins his first stable band "The Squires"
  • He then joins various musicians and collaborates around Winnepeg, leading to The Guess Who infamizing his song "Flying on the Ground is Wrong"
  • Not long after beginning as a solo artist in 1965, Neil relocates to L.A to form Buffalo Springfield
  • After Buffalo Springfield, Neil forms Crazy Horse with other talented musicians and records famous songs such as "Cinnamon Girl"
  • In 1968 Neil joins Crosby, Stills, Nash, Young, a formidable musical collaboration
  • In 1970-1972 he records "After the Gold Rush" and "Harvest"
  • In 1978 he records and tours the album "Rust Never Sleeps"
  • In the 1980s he records lesser known experimental music
  • In the 1990s he has a resurgence doing tours with many popular contemporary bands as well as recording "Harvest Moon" in 1992 and in 1995 he records the entrancing soundtrack for the film "Dead Man"
  • In the 2000s until the present Neil remains actively touring, recording new singles, and collaborating with various artists
